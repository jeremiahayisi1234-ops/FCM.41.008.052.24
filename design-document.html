VR Neoclassical Mansion (A-Frame) — Design & Technical Report

Project folder: Examination/  |  Engine: A-Frame 1.4.2  |  Extensions: aframe-extras (movement)

1. Design goals and architectural inspiration

The experience models a symmetrical neoclassical residence inspired by the White House layout: a dominant central block, east and west wings, and a columned entrance portico with a pediment. The composition emphasizes bilateral symmetry, clear axial paths from the main gate through the grounds to the portico, and a private rear zone for the swimming pool and animated figures.

The compound is enclosed by a perimeter fence with a dedicated main gate at the south approach. Pathways use contrasting stone-toned materials on the lawn plane to reinforce hierarchy and wayfinding without heavy textures, keeping GPU memory and bandwidth low.

2. Interior program (six developed rooms)

The following spaces are fully laid out with flooring, walls, scaled furniture, and decorative props:

  • Living room 1 (west): paired sofas, coffee table, side cabinet, wall art, rug, and a potted plant.
  • Living room 2 (east): alternate palette, matching furniture typology for variety.
  • Dining area: dining table with four seats, wall art, and sheer curtain planes.
  • Kitchen: cabinet run, counter, appliance block, and small plant.
  • Bedroom 1 and bedroom 2 (upper level): beds with headboards, side storage, curtains, art, and plants.

A stair flight connects the ground floor to the second level; the first-floor ceiling is split around the stairwell to avoid clipping while preserving spatial readability.

3. Landscape and tree specification

Exactly three tree types are implemented with three instances each (nine trees total), using lightweight primitives rather than heavy external meshes:

  • Type A — Broadleaf: trunk cylinder plus overlapping spheres for canopy volume.
  • Type B — Conical evergreen: stacked cones for a tiered silhouette.
  • Type C — Palm: tall trunk with angled cone fronds.

This satisfies the assignment constraint while maintaining stable frame times on typical classroom hardware.

4. Lighting model (entity-component system)

Lighting uses A-Frame’s declarative <a-entity light> components:

  • Ambient fill to prevent crushed shadows indoors.
  • Directional “sun” with shadow maps tuned for the plot size (orthographic frustum bounds on the shadow camera).
  • Point lights in living zones, dining, kitchen, bedrooms, and near the pool deck for dusk-like accents.

Shadow casting is enabled on the directional light and selected geometry to balance realism and performance; interior points omit cast shadows to limit overdraw and fill-rate cost.

5. Swimming pool and animated water

The pool uses a dedicated custom shader registered via AFRAME.registerShader('pool-water', …) in js/water-shader.js. The vertex stage displaces vertices with phase-shifted sine combinations; the fragment stage mixes deep and shallow tints, animated UV-phase patterns, and a light specular glint. A companion component pool-water-animate updates the time uniform each frame for continuous motion.

This approach avoids heavy physics simulation while still delivering believable surface motion suitable for real-time WebGL on a wide range of devices.

6. Animated children and dog

Two child groups and a dog are built from simple primitives (boxes and spheres) parented under animated rigs. Motion is implemented in js/pool-area-animations.js using parameterized paths:

  • Child 1: eased elliptical orbit with a light bounce for playfulness.
  • Child 2: inner loop with phase offset so the figures do not overlap exactly.
  • Dog: Gerono-style figure-eight path (parametric sin / sin·cos) for smooth, continuous laps near the pool deck.

Each frame, entities face their short lookahead position for coherent orientation.

7. Navigation and VR ergonomics

The camera rig uses a custom walk-controls component (js/walk-controls.js) so WASD and arrow keys move the viewer on the ground plane relative to the camera look direction, with look-controls on the camera for mouse or headset rotation. The canvas is focusable so keyboard input works reliably after clicking the scene.

8. Performance optimizations

  • Primitive-based architecture and furniture instead of multi-megabyte glTF scenes.
  • Shared materials via a-mixin to reduce material churn.
  • Shadow map resolution set to 1024×1024 as a practical default; selective shadow casters.
  • Linear fog to soften distant geometry and reduce perceived aliasing.
  • Renderer options: antialiasing, color management, and physically based lighting where supported.

9. Technical challenges and resolutions

9.1 Entrance visibility vs solid massing

A single bounding box for the central block occludes the interior visually and blocks coherent entry storytelling. The central volume was decomposed into back, side, and split front wall segments with a defined door opening and transom, preserving neoclassical proportions while allowing the foyer and stair to read correctly.

9.2 Water shader uniform updates

Custom shader materials may initialize after the first tick. The animation component lazily resolves mesh.material (including array materials) and updates time once uniforms are available.

9.3 Horizontal water plane ripples

Vertex displacement must use the plane’s in-plane axes (position.x / position.y for the default PlaneGeometry) so ripples move along the surface normal after the mesh is rotated flat.
